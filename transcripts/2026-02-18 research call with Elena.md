# Research call with Elena

Recorded Feb 18, 2026
Participants: [[Elena Voss]], me

## Notes

### Core question
What makes two ideas neighbors? Graph proximity is circular. Elena's hypothesis: shared cognitive context, not shared content.

### Key concept — "thinking weather"
Two notes written in the same headspace are more meaningfully related than two notes about the same topic written months apart. The atmospheric conditions when an idea was born matter more than the idea's content. [[James Okafor]] coined "thinking weather" independently in his newsletter.

### Connection to our work
Enzyme's entity extraction captures some of this through co-occurrence patterns. If two entities appear together across time, there's a contextual relationship even if the content is semantically different. Current embeddings miss temporal and emotional context — that's the gap.
