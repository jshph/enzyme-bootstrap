# Spatial interfaces for the vault explorer

**[[Priya Kapoor]]** [[2026-03-20]]

[[Marco Reyes]] mentioned you're looking at information architecture for the vault explorer. I've been thinking a lot about spatial interfaces for knowledge — the 2D canvas metaphor is overused but the alternatives (outlines, graphs) all have their own problems.

What if the primary interface wasn't visual at all? What if it was conversational, and the spatial view was secondary? Happy to sketch some ideas.

----

**me** [[2026-03-21]]

That's a provocation I needed to hear. We've been defaulting to a graph view because that's what the space expects, but honestly nobody works in graph views. They're beautiful in demos and useless in practice.

Conversational as default, spatial as the "pull up the map when you need orientation" mode. I like it. What would the third mode be? We've been thinking about three views.

----

**[[Priya Kapoor]]** [[2026-03-22]]

Three modes:

1. **Conversation** — ask and explore, this is the default
2. **Feed** — what changed and why it matters (not a dashboard, a feed)
3. **Map** — spatial overview for orientation, used sparingly

The feed is the new idea. It comes from what [[James Okafor]]'s community keeps saying: people don't want dashboards, they want awareness. A feed gives you that passive sense of what's happening without requiring you to go looking.

I'd want to talk to Marco before I propose a full redesign — understanding the investor lens on UX differentiation matters.
