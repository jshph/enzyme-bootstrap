# Knowledge neighborhoods

**[[Priya Kapoor]]** [[2026-02-15]]

Went down a rabbit hole on [[Elena Voss]]'s research after our call. Her framework for "knowledge neighborhoods" is exactly what's missing from most PKM tools — the idea that notes exist in relationship to each other, not just in folders.

Reminds me of what [[James Okafor]] keeps saying about his community: people don't want better organization, they want better retrieval.

----

**me** [[2026-02-16]]

That's the same thread Elena and I have been pulling on. Her core question is what actually makes two ideas "neighbors" — graph proximity is circular because you're just restating the link structure.

Her answer is shared cognitive context. Two notes written in the same headspace are more related than two notes about the same topic written months apart. James coined a phrase for it independently in his newsletter — "thinking weather."

----

**[[Priya Kapoor]]** [[2026-02-17]]

"Thinking weather" is a beautiful phrase. That maps to something I've been noticing in design research — when I cluster user interview quotes, the ones that feel like they belong together aren't always semantically similar. They were just said with the same energy, in response to the same kind of prompt.

If you could capture that computationally you'd have something no other tool has.
